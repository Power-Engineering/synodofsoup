About the Ledger

Charitable, accurate, accessible.

Synod of Soup exists for one reason: to let each Christian tradition speak its own conviction, from its own confessional documents, in its own theological idiom — without caricature.

We are not adjudicators. The Ledger is not a verdict. It is a witness stand, where the Council of Trent and the Westminster Confession, the Confession of Dositheus and the Schleitheim Articles, each get their hearing in full. When traditions disagree, the disagreement is preserved exactly — not flattened into false agreement, not sharpened into caricature.

The Synod is also a forum. Once you have read the historic positions, the threads under each tradition are yours: argue, question, defend, refine. The marketplace of theological reasoning is one of the oldest institutions in the Christian tradition. We are simply giving it a new room.

📜 Confessional citations

Every position quotes or paraphrases its own primary documents: Trent, Westminster, Augsburg, Heidelberg, Dort, the Thirty-Nine Articles, the Confession of Dositheus, the Schleitheim Confession, the Baptist Faith & Message, and many more.

📖 Scripture in full

Each debate lists the key biblical passages adduced by all sides — and explains how different hermeneutical traditions read the same texts to opposite ends.

🕊️ Civil dialogue

The comment threads are for theological reasoning, not for personal attack. We expect the dignity of disagreement — and remove what falls below it.

Our method

How the Ledger is built

01
Primary sources first

We cite confessions, conciliar decrees, papal documents, and patristic writings directly. No second-hand polemics, no straw men.

02
Each tradition in its idiom

Catholics speak of transubstantiation; the Orthodox of theosis; Lutherans of sola fide. We let each speak its own language.

03
Scripture for all sides

Every key biblical passage adduced by any tradition is listed. Different schools read the same verses to opposite conclusions — and we show how.

04
Historical context

Every debate is set in its historical frame: when it broke out, who shaped it, which councils settled or failed to settle it.

05
Living conversation

Each tradition has its own discussion thread. Argue, defend, refine — the marketplace of theological reasoning is one of the oldest institutions in Christianity.

06
Charity, not caricature

If a tradition would not recognise itself in our summary, we have failed. We revise as readers — especially insiders — flag inaccuracies.
